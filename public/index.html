Asbestos — once called "the magic mineral" for its remarkable properties: fire-resistant, strong, flexible, and abundant. It found its way into everything from insulation to brake pads, becoming a symbol of industrial progress and modern convenience. Today, we know asbestos carries a darker legacy. But the name remains, a play on words that acknowledges both the material's industrial mythology and its complex reality. Los Asbestos is music that works with those contradictions — generative, industrial, sometimes beautiful, sometimes unsettling, always evolving. Like the mineral itself, these tracks are built from layers: synthetic textures, generative patterns, digital artifacts that accumulate over time. They resist easy categorization, finding strength in their hybrid nature.
